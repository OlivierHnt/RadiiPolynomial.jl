Norms

The spaces introduced in the section Vector spaces are not normed a priori. Indeed, to satisfy the Radii Polynomial Theorem, it is useful to tune the Banach space on the fly.

All Banach spaces mentioned below are a subtype of the abstract type BanachSpace.

$\ell^1$ and $\ell^\infty$

Let $\mathscr{I}$ be a set of indices such that $\mathscr{I} \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ for some $d \in \mathbb{N}$.

The $\ell^1$ space is defined as

\[\ell^1 := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, +\infty > | a |_{\ell^1} := \sum_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} | a_\alpha | \right\}.\]

and the $\ell^\infty$ space is defined as

\[\ell^\infty := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, | a |_{\ell^\infty} := \sup_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} | a_\alpha | < +\infty \right\}.\]

These Banach spaces are representing by the structures ℓ¹ (\ell<TAB>\^1<TAB>) and ℓ∞ (\ell<TAB>\infty<TAB>).

julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(2), [1.0, 2.0, 3.0]);
julia> norm(a, ℓ¹())6.0
julia> norm(a, ℓ∞())3.0

Weighted $\ell^1$

Let $\mathscr{I}$ be a set of indices such that $\mathscr{I} \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ for some $d \in \mathbb{N}$.

The weighted $\ell^1$ space is defined as

\[\ell^1_w := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, | a |_{\ell^1_\nu} := \sum_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} |a_\alpha| w_1(\alpha_1) \cdots w_d(\alpha_d) < +\infty \right\},\]

where, for $i = 1, \dots, d$, either:

  • $w_i$ is a sequence of geometric weights of rate $\nu_i > 0$ given by $w_i(\alpha_i) := \nu_i^{|\alpha_i|}$;
  • $w_i$ is a sequence of algebraic weights of rate $s_i \geq 0$ given by $w_i(\alpha_i) := (1 + |\alpha_i|)^{s_i}$.

Weightedℓ¹ (Weighted\ell<TAB>\^1<TAB>) represents a weighted $\ell^1$ space by wrapping GeometricWeights and AlgebraicWeights.

julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(2), [1.0, 2.0, 3.0]);
julia> norm(a, Weightedℓ¹(GeometricWeights(2.0)))17.0
julia> norm(a, Weightedℓ¹(AlgebraicWeights(2.0)))36.0
julia> b = Sequence(Taylor(2) ⊗ Fourier(2, 1.) ⊗ Chebyshev(2), ones(3*5*3));
julia> norm(b, Weightedℓ¹((GeometricWeights(0.2), GeometricWeights(1.2), AlgebraicWeights(0.9))))78.7121043576136

$H^s$

Let $\mathscr{I}$ be a set of indices such that $\mathscr{I} \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ for some $d \in \mathbb{N}$ and $s \in [1, +\infty)$. The Sobolev space $H^s$ is defined as

\[H^s := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, | a |_{H^s} := \left( \sum_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} | a_\alpha | \left( 1 + | \alpha_1 |^2 + \dots + | \alpha_d |^2 \right)^s \right)^{1/2} < +\infty \right\}.\]

Hˢ (H\^s<TAB>) represents the Sobolev space $H^s$ by wrapping the exponent $s$.

julia> a = Sequence(Fourier(1, 1.0), [0.5, 0.0, 0.5]);
julia> norm(a, Hˢ(2.0))1.4142135623730951

Normed cartesian space

For the norm of a CartesianSpace, one may use a NormedCartesianSpace to either:

  • use the same BanachSpace for each space.
  • use a different BanachSpace for each space.
julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(1)^2 × Chebyshev(1)^2, [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8]);
julia> inner = NormedCartesianSpace((Weightedℓ¹(GeometricWeights(2.0)), Weightedℓ¹(AlgebraicWeights(3.0))), ℓ∞());
julia> norm(a, NormedCartesianSpace(inner, ℓ¹()))170.0
