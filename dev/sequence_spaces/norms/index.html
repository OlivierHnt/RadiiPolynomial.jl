Norms

The spaces introduced in the section Vector spaces are not normed a priori. Indeed, to satisfy the Radii Polynomial Theorem, it is useful to tune the Banach space on the fly.

All Banach spaces mentioned below are a subtype of the abstract type BanachSpace.

$\ell^1$ and $\ell^\infty$

Let $\mathscr{I}$ be a set of indices such that $\mathscr{I} \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ for some $d \in \mathbb{N}$.

The $\ell^1$ space is defined as

\[\ell^1 := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, +\infty > | a |_{\ell^1} := \sum_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} | a_\alpha | \right\}.\]

and the $\ell^\infty$ space is defined as

\[\ell^\infty := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, +\infty > | a |_{\ell^\infty} := \sup_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} | a_\alpha | \right\}.\]

These Banach spaces are representing by the structures ℓ¹ (\ell<TAB>\^1<TAB>) and ℓ∞ (\ell<TAB>\infty<TAB>).

julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(2), [1.0, 2.0, 3.0])Sequence in Taylor(2) with coefficients Vector{Float64}:
 1.0
 2.0
 3.0
julia> norm(a, ℓ¹())6.0
julia> norm(a, ℓ∞())3.0

Geometric weights

Let $\mathscr{I}$ be a set of indices such that $\mathscr{I} \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ for some $d \in \mathbb{N}$.

The geometric weights of rate $\nu > 0$ are the numbers $\nu^{|\alpha|}$ for all $\alpha \in \mathscr{I}$. The corresponding weighted $\ell^1$ space is defined as

\[\ell^1_\nu := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, +\infty > | a |_{\ell^1_\nu} := \sum_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} |a_\alpha| \nu^{|\alpha|} \right\}.\]

julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(2), [1.0, 2.0, 3.0])Sequence in Taylor(2) with coefficients Vector{Float64}:
 1.0
 2.0
 3.0
julia> norm(a, Weightedℓ¹(GeometricWeights(2.0)))17.0

Note that such a Banach space may also yield a Banach algebra for a suitable choice of the rate $\nu$. Namely,

  • for Taylor, $\ell^1_\nu$ is a Banach algebra for all $\nu > 0$ .
  • for Fourier and Chebyshev, $\ell^1_\nu$ is a Banach algebra for all $\nu \geq 1$.
julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(2) ⊗ Fourier(2, 1.) ⊗ Chebyshev(2), ones(3*5*3));
julia> b = Sequence(Taylor(1) ⊗ Fourier(3, 1.) ⊗ Chebyshev(0), ones(2*7*1));
julia> X = Weightedℓ¹((GeometricWeights(0.2), GeometricWeights(1.2), GeometricWeights(2.0)));
julia> norm(a*b, X)1008.9644697600002
julia> norm(a, X) * norm(b, X)5913.6619776

Algebraic weights

Let $\mathscr{I}$ be a set of indices such that $\mathscr{I} \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ for some $d \in \mathbb{N}$.

The algebraic weights of rate $s \geq 0$ are the numbers $(1 + |\alpha|)^s$ for all $\alpha \in \mathscr{I}$. The corresponding weighted $\ell^1$ space is defined as

\[\ell^1_s := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, +\infty > | a |_{\ell^1_s} := \sum_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} |a_\alpha| (1 + |\alpha|)^s \right\}.\]

julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(2), [1.0, 2.0, 3.0])Sequence in Taylor(2) with coefficients Vector{Float64}:
 1.0
 2.0
 3.0
julia> norm(a, Weightedℓ¹(AlgebraicWeights(2.0)))36.0

Note that such a Banach space may also yield a Banach algebra.

julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(2) ⊗ Fourier(2, 1.) ⊗ Chebyshev(2), ones(3*5*3));
julia> b = Sequence(Taylor(1) ⊗ Fourier(3, 1.) ⊗ Chebyshev(0), ones(2*7*1));
julia> X = Weightedℓ¹((AlgebraicWeights(0.2), AlgebraicWeights(1.2), AlgebraicWeights(2.0)));
julia> norm(a*b, X)25776.784295473244
julia> norm(a, X) * norm(b, X)182411.3331291946

$H^s$

Let $\mathscr{I}$ be a set of indices such that $\mathscr{I} \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ for some $d \in \mathbb{N}$ and $s \in [1, +\infty)$. The Sobolev space $H^s$ is defined as

\[H^s := \left\{ a \in \mathbb{C}^\mathscr{I} \, : \, +\infty > | a |_{H^s} := \left( \sum_{\alpha \in \mathscr{I}} | a_\alpha | \left( 1 + \sum_{i=1}^d | \alpha_i |^2 \right)^s \right)^{1/2} \right\}.\]

The 𝐻ˢ (\itH<TAB>\^s<TAB>) wraps such a $s$.

julia> a = Sequence(Fourier(1, 1.0), [0.5, 0.0, 0.5])Sequence in Fourier{Float64}(1, 1.0) with coefficients Vector{Float64}:
 0.5
 0.0
 0.5
julia> norm(a, 𝐻ˢ(2.0))1.4142135623730951

Normed cartesian space

For the norm of a CartesianSpace, one may use a NormedCartesianSpace to either:

  • use the same BanachSpace for each space.
  • use a different BanachSpace for each space.
julia> a = Sequence(Taylor(1)^2 × Chebyshev(1)^2, [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8])Sequence in Taylor(1)² × Chebyshev(1)² with coefficients Vector{Int64}:
 1
 2
 3
 4
 5
 6
 7
 8
julia> inner = NormedCartesianSpace((Weightedℓ¹(GeometricWeights(2.0)), Weightedℓ¹(AlgebraicWeights(3.0))), ℓ∞())NormedCartesianSpace{Tuple{Weightedℓ¹{GeometricWeights{Float64}}, Weightedℓ¹{AlgebraicWeights{Float64}}}, ℓ∞}((Weightedℓ¹{GeometricWeights{Float64}}(GeometricWeights{Float64}(2.0)), Weightedℓ¹{AlgebraicWeights{Float64}}(AlgebraicWeights{Float64}(3.0))), ℓ∞())
julia> norm(a, NormedCartesianSpace(inner, ℓ¹()))170.0
