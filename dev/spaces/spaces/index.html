Spaces

The spaces under considerations are the subspaces of $\ell^1_{\mathbb{S}_1 \times \dots \times \mathbb{S}_d,\nu}$ such that their elements are compactly supported sequences. The abstract type SequenceSpace has the two subtypes UnivariateSpace and TensorSpace where the latter corresponds to the tensor products ⊗ of some spaces in UnivariateSpace.

Each UnivariateSpace comes with a field order beyond which the coefficients of all sequences in this space are zero. Naturally, one can take the union ∪ and the intersection ∩ of some UnivariateSpace.

Taylor

A Taylor sequence space is a truncated Taylor space which essentially amounts to a univariate polynomial of a prescribed order $n$. The ordered basis under consideration is $\{\phi_0, \dots, \phi_n\}$ where $\phi_k(t) \doteqdot t^k$ for $k = 0, \dots, n$. This is the main sequence space when dealing with analytic functions.

Fourier

A Fourier sequence space is a truncated Fourier space of a prescribed order $n$ and frequency $\omega$. The ordered basis under consideration is $\{\phi_{-n}, \dots, \phi_n\}$ where $\phi_k(t) \doteqdot e^{i \omega k t}$ for $k = -n, \dots, n$. This is the main sequence space when dealing with periodic functions.

Chebyshev

A Chebyshev sequence space is a truncated Chebyshev space of a prescribed order $n$. The ordered basis under consideration is $\{\phi_0, \dots, \phi_n\}$ where $\phi_k(\cos(\theta)) \doteqdot \cos(k\theta)$ for $k = 0, \dots, n$.

Tensor space

A TensorSpace is the tensor product of some UnivariateSpace. The ordered basis under consideration is generated from the ordered basis of each UnivariateSpace.

julia> using RadiiPolynomial

julia> taylor = Taylor(2)
Taylor(2)

julia> fourier = Fourier(1, 1.)
Fourier{Float64}(1, 1.0)

julia> taylor ⊗ fourier
Taylor(2) ⨂ Fourier{Float64}(1, 1.0)

julia> eachindex(taylor ⊗ fourier)
9-element Array{Tuple{Int64,Int64},1}:
 (0, -1)
 (1, -1)
 (2, -1)
 (0, 0)
 (1, 0)
 (2, 0)
 (0, 1)
 (1, 1)
 (2, 1)
